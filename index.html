My name is Gabriele Cimolino. I will soon be entering my fourth year of studying Computing at Queen's University. Before this one, I completed an arts degree with a major in Classical Studies. These seven years have given me many opportunities to explore the world, and a unique perspective that is informed by my own experience of our shared ideas.

Right now, I am seeking projects and places to be a part of. I enjoy making things, and want to find the people who are making the best things so that their knowledge and guidance might improve my craft.

Typically, I make software. This has always been the case, but the work that I do often requires me to develop other skills. When I make games, there are sprites and textures to be drawn and music to be arranged. Projects need webpages, like this one, so I'll do that too. Occasionally, specialized hardware is required, for things such as IOT devices, so I'll get out the breadboard and wire strippers.

The source files for many of my creations can be found on  Github . As well, I have a game that can be played in browser at  itch.io . I publish the project files for game music that I make to the ModArchive under the name  AnnaMae . You can listen to the tunes I've made by hitting the play button on the audio player at the top of each of this site's pages.

Oui, Chef!!

Oui, Chef!! is a machine learning game with a restaurant management theme. As far as I know, it is the first of its kind. The player must prepare dishes by selecting the correct ingredients, assembling them, and sending the order out. To help with this, the player can hire employees to do the same task. Each employee has a neural network that learns how to more accurately select correct ingredients when told to remake an order.

This game was made as a term project for my game design class. I had the help of Sam Lee and Quentin Petraroia in making it. Oui, Chef!! can be played in browser, without the ability to save progress, at itch.io.

Float Blasters

Float Blasters is a space exploration game inspired by Metroid. Most of what is there so far has been created by my friend  Eric Thompson  as his capstone project at Humber.

Our plan now is to continue development and flesh out the amazing work he has already done. I have been entrusted with developing a new system architecture, creating more sophisticated enemy AI that uses machine learning, and composing the music, with Eric on guitar.

The demo features my first attempt at making more interesting enemies. The Gorbons, black and yellow spheres that chase the player and explode when close enough, use Craig Reynold's Boids algorithm to produce flocking behaviour. Upon launching the Gorbonia scene from the main menu, the Gorbons will group up to form unnerving swarms of destruction. Much has yet to be done, including the design and implementation of another enemy type with multiple modes of behaviour, but Boids will feature prominently throughout the user's experience of the finished game.
